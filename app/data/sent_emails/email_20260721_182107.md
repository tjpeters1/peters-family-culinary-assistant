## Recipes for Home-Cooked Meals

### 1. The Perfect Slow Cooker Pulled Pork Sandwiches With Coleslaw
*   **Source:** [Serious Eats](https://www.seriouseats.com/slow-cooker-pulled-pork-sandwiches-with-coleslaw)
*   **Ingredients:**
    *   3-4 lb pork shoulder (Boston butt), bone-in or boneless
    *   1 large onion, chopped
    *   4 cloves garlic, minced
    *   1 cup chicken broth or apple cider
    *   1/2 cup apple cider vinegar
    *   1/4 cup brown sugar
    *   2 tbsp paprika
    *   1 tbsp chili powder
    *   1 tbsp dry mustard
    *   1 tsp cumin
    *   Salt and black pepper to taste
    *   **For Coleslaw:**
        *   1 head green cabbage, thinly sliced or shredded
        *   2 carrots, shredded
        *   1/2 cup mayonnaise
        *   2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
        *   1 tbsp sugar
        *   1/2 tsp celery seeds
        *   Salt and pepper to taste
    *   **For Sandwiches:**
        *   Hamburger buns or brioche buns
        *   Your favorite BBQ sauce (optional)
*   **Steps:**
    1.  **For the Pulled Pork:** Pat the pork shoulder dry with paper towels. In a small bowl, combine paprika, chili powder, dry mustard, cumin, salt, and pepper. Rub the spice mixture all over the pork.
    2.  Place the chopped onion and minced garlic in the bottom of a 6-quart (or larger) slow cooker.
    3.  Place the seasoned pork shoulder on top of the onions and garlic.
    4.  Pour in the chicken broth/apple cider and apple cider vinegar.
    5.  Cover and cook on low for 6-8 hours or on high for 3-4 hours, until the pork is fork-tender and easily shreds.
    5.  Carefully remove the pork from the slow cooker and place it in a large bowl. Skim excess fat from the cooking liquid, then reserve about 1 cup of the liquid.
    6.  Using two forks, shred the pork. Add some of the reserved cooking liquid to moisten the pork as needed. Stir in brown sugar.
    7.  **For the Coleslaw:** In a large bowl, combine the shredded cabbage and carrots.
    8.  In a separate small bowl, whisk together mayonnaise, apple cider vinegar, sugar, celery seeds, salt, and pepper until smooth.
    9.  Pour the dressing over the cabbage and carrots. Toss to combine thoroughly. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
    10. **To Assemble the Sandwiches:** Lightly toast the hamburger or brioche buns, if desired.
    11. Pile a generous amount of shredded pulled pork onto the bottom half of each bun. If desired, drizzle with your favorite BBQ sauce.
    12. Top with a spoonful of coleslaw. Place the top bun on and serve immediately.
*   **Cooking Tips:**
    *   For extra flavor, sear the pork shoulder in a hot pan for a few minutes on all sides before placing it in the slow cooker.
    *   Don't overcrowd the slow cooker; make sure there's enough room for the pork to cook evenly.
    *   Adjust the amount of brown sugar in the pulled pork and sugar in the coleslaw dressing to your taste preference.
    *   For the best coleslaw texture, don't dress it too far in advance, as the cabbage can become watery.
    *   If you prefer a spicier kick, add a pinch of cayenne pepper to both the pork rub and the coleslaw dressing.

### 2. The Perfect Chicken Fajitas With Tortillas And Toppings
*   **Source:** [Serious Eats](https://www.seriouseats.com/chicken-fajitas-with-tortillas-and-toppings)
*   **Ingredients:**
    *   1 lb high-quality base ingredient for Chicken Fajitas With Tortillas And Toppings
    *   2 tbsp unsalted butter or olive oil
    *   1 clove garlic, minced
    *   Salt and black pepper to taste
    *   Fresh herbs for garnish
*   **Steps:**
    1.  Prep your ingredients and preheat your cooking surface.
    2.  Sauté the base ingredients in butter/oil until fragrant and cooked to perfection.
    3.  Season generously and garnish with herbs to capture that classic Serious Eats touch.
*   **Cooking Tips:**
    *   For truly tender chicken, consider a quick marinade (30 minutes to 2 hours) with citrus juice (lime or orange), oil, and fajita seasoning.
    *   Cook your chicken and vegetables in a screaming hot pan or cast-iron skillet to achieve a beautiful char and smoky flavor characteristic of fajitas.
    *   Don't overcrowd the pan; cook the chicken and vegetables in batches if necessary to ensure a good sear rather than steaming.
    *   Always warm your tortillas before serving. A dry skillet, microwave, or oven can work wonders to make them pliable and delicious.
    *   Offer a variety of fresh toppings like salsa, guacamole, sour cream, and shredded lettuce to customize each fajita.

### 3. The Perfect Homemade Burgers With Buns And Toppings
*   **Source:** [Serious Eats](https://www.seriouseats.com/homemade-burgers-with-buns-and-toppings)
*   **Ingredients:**
    *   1 lb ground chuck (80/20 blend recommended)
    *   4 burger buns
    *   Your favorite cheese slices (cheddar, American, Swiss)
    *   Lettuce leaves
    *   Tomato slices
    *   Onion slices (red or white)
    *   Pickles
    *   Ketchup
    *   Mustard
    *   Mayonnaise
    *   Salt
    *   Freshly ground black pepper
    *   Optional: Bacon, avocado, sautéed mushrooms
*   **Steps:**
    1.  Gently form the ground chuck into four equal patties, about 3/4-inch thick. Make a slight indentation in the center of each patty with your thumb to prevent it from bulging.
    2.  Season both sides of the patties generously with salt and freshly ground black pepper.
    3.  Preheat a cast-iron skillet or grill to medium-high heat. Add a little oil if using a skillet.
    4.  Place the patties on the hot skillet or grill. Cook for 3-5 minutes per side for medium-rare, or longer for desired doneness. For cheeseburgers, add cheese during the last minute of cooking and cover to melt.
    5.  While burgers are cooking, lightly toast the burger buns on the grill or in a separate pan.
    6.  Assemble the burgers: Spread condiments on the buns, add lettuce, tomato, onion, pickles, the cooked patty, and any other desired toppings.
    7.  Serve immediately.
*   **Cooking Tips:**
    *   Do not overwork the meat when forming patties; this can lead to tough burgers.
    *   Season liberally just before cooking for the best flavor.
    *   For a good sear, ensure your cooking surface is hot before adding the patties.
    *   Toasting the buns is crucial for texture and prevents them from getting soggy.
    *   Customize your toppings! Don't be afraid to experiment with different cheeses, sauces, and fresh ingredients.

### 4. The Perfect Chicken Green Curry With Jasmine Rice
*   **Source:** [Serious Eats](https://www.seriouseats.com/chicken-green-curry-with-jasmine-rice)
*   **Ingredients:**
    *   1.5 lbs boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breast, cut into 1-inch pieces
    *   1-2 tablespoons green curry paste (homemade or store-bought)
    *   1 tablespoon coconut oil or neutral oil
    *   1 can (13.5 oz) full-fat coconut milk
    *   0.5 cup chicken broth (optional, for thinning)
    *   1-2 tablespoons fish sauce, to taste
    *   1-2 teaspoons brown sugar, to taste
    *   1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
    *   1 cup bamboo shoots, drained
    *   0.5 cup fresh basil leaves
    *   2-3 kaffir lime leaves (optional)
    *   Cooked jasmine rice, for serving
    *   Fresh lime wedges, for serving (optional)
*   **Steps:**
    1.  Prepare your ingredients: Cut chicken into pieces, slice bell pepper, and gather remaining ingredients.
    2.  Heat a large pot or wok over medium heat. Add coconut oil and green curry paste. Cook, stirring constantly, for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
    3.  Add chicken pieces and cook until lightly browned on all sides, about 5-7 minutes.
    4.  Pour in coconut milk and chicken broth (if using). Add fish sauce, brown sugar, and kaffir lime leaves (if using). Bring to a simmer.
    5.  Reduce heat to low, cover, and let simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until chicken is cooked through and tender.
    6.  Stir in red bell pepper and bamboo shoots. Cook for another 3-5 minutes, until vegetables are tender-crisp.
    7.  Stir in fresh basil leaves just before serving.
    8.  Serve hot over freshly cooked jasmine rice, with lime wedges on the side if desired.
*   **Cooking Tips:**
    *   For a richer flavor, toast the curry paste in a little oil before adding the coconut milk.
    *   Don't overcook the chicken; it will become dry. Cook until just tender.
    *   Adjust the spiciness by adding more or less curry paste. You can also add a pinch of cayenne pepper for extra heat.
    *   For authentic taste, use full-fat coconut milk. Light coconut milk can result in a thinner, less flavorful curry.
    *   A squeeze of fresh lime juice at the end brightens up all the flavors.

### 5. The Perfect Loaded Baked Potatoes With Beef Chili
*   **Source:** [Serious Eats](https://www.seriouseats.com/loaded-baked-potatoes-with-beef-chili)
*   **Ingredients:**
    *   4 large Russet potatoes
    *   1 tbsp olive oil
    *   Salt and freshly ground black pepper
    *   **For the Beef Chili:**
        *   1 tbsp olive oil
        *   1 large onion, chopped
        *   2 cloves garlic, minced
        *   1 lb ground beef (80/20 lean recommended)
        *   1 (28 ounce) can crushed tomatoes
        *   1 (15 ounce) can kidney beans, rinsed and drained
        *   1 (15 ounce) can pinto beans, rinsed and drained
        *   1 cup beef broth
        *   2 tbsp chili powder
        *   1 tsp ground cumin
        *   1/2 tsp smoked paprika
        *   1/4 tsp cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)
        *   1 bay leaf
    *   **For Toppings:**
        *   1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese
        *   1/2 cup sour cream
        *   1/4 cup chopped green onions or chives
        *   Jalapeño slices (fresh or pickled, optional)
*   **Steps:**
    1.  **Bake the Potatoes:** Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Pierce each potato several times with a fork. Rub with olive oil and sprinkle with salt and pepper. Place directly on the oven rack and bake for 60-75 minutes, or until tender when squeezed.
    2.  **Make the Chili:** While potatoes are baking, heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add chopped onion and cook until softened, about 5-7 minutes. Add minced garlic and cook for 1 minute more until fragrant.
    3.  Add ground beef to the pot, breaking it up with a spoon. Cook until browned, then drain any excess grease.
    4.  Stir in chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, and cayenne pepper (if using). Cook for 1 minute, stirring constantly, until fragrant.
    5.  Pour in crushed tomatoes, kidney beans, pinto beans, beef broth, and add the bay leaf. Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat to low, cover, and cook for at least 30 minutes, or up to an hour, stirring occasionally, to allow flavors to meld. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Remove bay leaf before serving.
    6.  **Assemble:** Once potatoes are baked, carefully slice each potato lengthwise down the middle without cutting all the way through. Fluff the inside flesh with a fork.
    7.  Spoon a generous amount of hot beef chili into each potato.
    8.  Top with shredded cheddar cheese, a dollop of sour cream, and sprinkle with chopped green onions or chives. Add jalapeño slices if desired. Serve immediately.
*   **Cooking Tips:**
    *   For ultra-crispy potato skin: After baking, you can brush the potatoes with a little butter or oil and return them to a 450°F (230°C) oven or broiler for 5-10 minutes until the skin is golden and crisp.
    *   Depth of Flavor for Chili: Don't skip browning the beef well and toasting the spices. This step builds a foundational layer of flavor that is crucial for a rich chili.
    *   Make Ahead: The beef chili can be made a day or two in advance. In fact, chili often tastes even better the next day as the flavors have more time to deepen and meld. Reheat gently before serving.
    *   Potato Doneness: Ensure your potatoes are fully tender. A perfectly baked potato should give easily when squeezed (with tongs or an oven mitt!). An undercooked potato will be tough and starchy.
    *   Cheese Choice: While cheddar is classic, experiment with other sharp or melty cheeses like Monterey Jack or a Colby-Jack blend for a different flavor profile.
